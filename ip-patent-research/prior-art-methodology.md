# Life Sciences Prior Art Search Methodology
**Author:** [Amit Shrishrimal/ Pharmaceutical IP Specialist]

This document outlines the standard operating procedure (SOP) I utilize to conduct robust prior art searches for small molecules, biological therapeutics, and pharmaceutical formulations. This methodology ensures maximum coverage across patent and non-patent literature (NPL) to assess novelty and patentability.

## 1. Search Strategy & Database Selection
To ensure comprehensive data capture, searches are executed across a blend of global patent registries and scientific literature databases:

### Patent Databases
*   **Google Patents:** For rapid indexing, semantic searching, and citation mapping.
*   **Espacenet (EPO):** For advanced European and worldwide patent family tracking.
*   **WIPO Patentscope:** For tracking international PCT applications.

### Non-Patent Literature (NPL) Databases
*   **PubMed / MEDLINE:** For clinical trial data, pharmacology studies, and biomedical literature.
*   **PubChem:** To cross-reference IUPAC names, chemical structures, and SMILES strings.

## 2. Keywords & Classification Matrix
Searches are structured using precise Boolean operators `(AND, OR, NOT)` combined with Cooperative Patent Classifications (CPC).

### Target Classifications (Life Sciences Focus)
*   **A61K:** Preparations for medical, dental, or toilet purposes (Formulations).
*   **A61P:** Specific therapeutic activity of chemical compounds or medicinal preparations.
*   **C07D:** Heterocyclic compounds (Small molecule drugs).

## 3. Step-by-Step Execution Workflow
1. **Identify the Core Inventive Step:** Break down the target pharmaceutical asset into its structural components (e.g., active moiety, specific excipients, polymorphic form, or specific mechanism of action).
2. **Build Keyword Strings:** Generate synonyms combining the chemical name, generic name, laboratory codes, and therapeutic targets.
3. **Execute Broad to Narrow Searches:** Begin with wide classification-based queries, then narrow down using specific keyword combinations to filter out irrelevant records.
4. **Analyze the Hit List:** Review independent claims of the most relevant patent hits and read abstracts of key biomedical journals.
5. **Map to a Novelty Matrix:** Document findings detailing how prior disclosures overlap with or differ from the target asset.
